My Projects

Gin Rummy

In this OCaml project, I collaborated with two classmates to develop a Gin Rummy game that involved an interactive GUI and multiple player options, including the number of players, number of cards, theme, and more. I focused on creating the Deck module that provided shuffling and dealing functionality and the Auto Player module that used game logic to provide three levels of difficulty for a single player. The image to the left displays the GUI during game play. In this specific example, player1's 7-card hand is shown, and it is their turn to choose a card by clicking on the desired pile.

New York Times News App

In this iOS app development project, I utilized Xcode and the New York Times API to create an application that allows users to search for articles by keyword and view summaries of the results. In the image to the left, the first screen depicts the search functionality and the news articles that appear in the results based on the given search. The second screen shows the summary of a specific article after it has been selected, which includes the article title, a blurb, a photo, and a link to the article.

RISCV Processor

I used Logisim to develop a RISCV processor that is made up of five key stages, fetch, decode, execute, memory, and writeback, and the connections between them. The processor takes in a series of instructions and outputs the specified value to the specified location. The image to the left depicts all of the elements of this processor and the circuits and wires that connect them.

Space Invaders

I collaborated with a partner to program a simple version of the Space Invaders game in Python. As displayed in the image to the left, this game involves a space shuttle that moves side to side and tries to shoot the aliens before losing all of its lives. We also added sound effects and the functionality to keep track of the score.
